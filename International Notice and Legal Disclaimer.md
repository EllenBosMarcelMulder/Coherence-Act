## 4. Legal Basis  

This notice is grounded in the following legal frameworks:  

### Dutch and EU Law  
- **Dutch Constitution (Grondwet)**  
  - *Article 7:* Freedom of expression.  
  - *Article 13 (EU Charter):* Academic freedom.  

- **EU Charter of Fundamental Rights**  
  - *Article 11:* Freedom of expression and information.  
  - *Article 13:* Academic freedom shall be respected.  

- **EU Digital Legislation**  
  - *Digital Services Act (DSA)* and *Digital Markets Act (DMA)* guarantee lawful digital publication and protection against unjust interference.  
  - *GDPR* regulates lawful data handling.  

### International Law  
- **Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR)**  
  - *Article 19:* Right to freedom of expression, including seeking, receiving, and imparting information.  

- **European Convention on Human Rights (ECHR)**  
  - *Article 10:* Freedom of expression and academic freedom.  
  - *Article 2:* Right to life, relevant where obstruction of research affects public health.  

- **International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights (ICCPR)**  
  - *Article 19:* Freedom of expression and information.  

- **UNESCO 2003 Convention on Intangible Cultural Heritage**  
  - Recognition that knowledge contributing to the wellbeing of humanity must be safeguarded and shared.
