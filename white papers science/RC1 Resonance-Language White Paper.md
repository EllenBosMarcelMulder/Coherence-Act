## Historical Context: Language as Energy and Quantum Coherence

### The Evolution of Language-Energy Concepts

The conceptualization of **language as a physical energy phenomenon** has deep historical roots.  

- **Ancient Sanskrit texts** described *AUM* as the primordial sound of universe creation, with 35 of 49 letters structured to produce *“soft” resonant sounds* (1).  
- The 19th-century linguist **Max Müller’s “ding-dong theory”** proposed that all things possess natural vibrating resonance, echoed by humans in their earliest words — a concept that resonates with contemporary understanding of **acoustic phonetics** and **formant frequencies** (2).  

Modern research validates aspects of these historical perspectives through **rigorous acoustic analysis** (3,4):  

- The **source–filter model** of speech production, established by **Fant (1960)** and developed further by **Stevens (1998)**, demonstrates that voiced sounds originate from vocal fold vibrations, passing through the vocal tract, which acts as an **acoustic filter** creating formant frequencies fundamental to speech perception (3,4,5).  
- Typical adult male vocal tracts (~17 cm) create resonances at approximately:  
  - F1 ≈ 500 Hz  
  - F2 ≈ 1500 Hz  
  - F3 ≈ 2500 Hz  
  These provide the **physical basis** for language production (5).

**Proposed mechanism:**

- Recent publications suggest 40Hz stimulation may increase peptide release from interneurons, potentially supporting **amyloid clearance** via the brain’s **glymphatic system** (12).

*Note: These results, while promising, require larger-scale trials and peer review before clinical implementation.*
